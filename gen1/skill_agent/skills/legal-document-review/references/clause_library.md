# Legal Clause Library

## Indemnification
**What it means**: One party agrees to cover the other's losses, damages, or legal costs.
**Watch for**: Broad, one-sided indemnification with no cap or carve-outs.
**Red flag**: "Party A shall indemnify Party B for any and all claims..." (unlimited scope)
**Standard**: Mutual indemnification, limited to direct damages from proven breach.

## Limitation of Liability
**What it means**: Caps the maximum damages one party can claim.
**Watch for**: Liability excluded entirely, or cap set below contract value.
**Red flag**: "In no event shall either party be liable for any damages whatsoever."
**Standard**: Cap = 12 months of fees paid; excludes gross negligence and IP infringement.

## IP Assignment
**What it means**: Transfers ownership of created work to one party.
**Watch for**: Broad assignment with no carve-out for pre-existing IP.
**Red flag**: "All inventions, discoveries, and works created by contractor are owned by client."
**Standard**: Client owns deliverables; contractor retains pre-existing IP and tools.

## Non-Compete
**What it means**: Restricts one party from working with competitors.
**Watch for**: Overly broad scope (industry-wide), long duration (> 1 year), wide geography.
**Red flag**: "Contractor shall not work in the software industry for 3 years globally."

## Auto-Renewal
**What it means**: Contract automatically renews unless cancelled within notice period.
**Watch for**: Short notice windows (< 30 days), long renewal periods.
**Red flag**: "Agreement renews annually unless cancelled 90 days prior in writing."
**Standard**: 30-day notice, month-to-month renewal option.

## Governing Law
**What it means**: Specifies which country/state law applies and where disputes are heard.
**Watch for**: Jurisdiction far from your location, arbitration clauses that waive jury rights.
**Red flag**: Mandatory arbitration in foreign jurisdiction with no appeal rights.
**Standard**: Mutual agreement on neutral jurisdiction; option for local courts.
